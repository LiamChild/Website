What Is Sydney Metro?

    Sydney Metro is a rapid transit system in Sydney, Australia, and is the largest public transport project in the country. It is designed to improve the cities public transport network by providing high-frequency, driverless trains that operate independently of the existing suburban rail network. Sydney Metro aims to reduce travel times, increase capacity, and improve connectivity across the Greater Sydney area.

    The system currently has one line which is the M1 Metro North West & Bankstown Line. Future expansions, including the Metro West Line and the Metro Western Sydney Airport Line, are also planned to connect additional parts of the city and its suburbs.

Current Sydney Metro Line

    Sydeney Metro so far only has one line which is the M1 Metro North West & Bankstown Line. The line runs 52km through tunnels, and bridges. It is comprised of 21 stations. The line runs 6 car Alstom Metropolis EMU's (Electric Multiple Umit). Headways range from 4 minutes in the peak, 5-7minutes in the intra peak, and 10 minutes off peak and weekends.

Future Expansion

    Sydney Metro is currently in the process of building 3 projects. Two new lines and in the pocess of converting an existng train line into Metro. The first to be finished will be the conversion of the Bankstown line to Metro and will connect with the existing M1 Metro line. The Bankstown metro conversion should be finished and services running by 2025. The next to be finished would be the Western Sydney Metro and Arostroplis Metro line which would connect the existing St Mary's station to the new Nancy Bird Walton Western Sydney Airport. This line is expected to open in 2026 alongside with the airport opening. The last metro project to open would be the Metro West line. The line would make and easy connection from Sydney CBD to Parramatta, which is to be proposed as the Sydney's second CBD. The line would run from a new Hunter Street Station and then would go though Pyrmont, The Bays, Five Dock, Burwood North, then would connect with the existing Nth Strathfield Station and then would connect with existing Olympic Station, and then would go to a seperate Parramatta Station and then would finish its journey at the existing Westemead Station. The line has been proposed to finish in 2030.

    The government has also proposed that they will build a new metro line every 4-6 years for 100 years.
